Body mass index - BMI

BMI, formerly called the Quetelet index, is a measure for indicating nutritional status in adults. It is defined as a person’s weight in kilograms divided by the square of the person’s height in metres (kg/m2) for both men and women. For example, an adult who weighs 60 kg and whose height is 1.80 m will have a BMI of 18.22 .

Note. BMI is derived from a person’s weight in kilograms, divided by height (squared) in metres. The recommended levels are adapted from the global WHO recommendation of 18.5–24.9 as a normal BMI.

BMI Classification
Below 18.5 Under Weight
18.5–24.9 Normal weight
25.0–29.9 Over Weight
30.0–34.9 Obesity class I
35.0–39.9 Obesity class II
Above 40 Obesity class III
